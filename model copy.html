MORE THAN JUST A TOILET

Sustainable Design

Eco-Toilets have been designed by looking far into the future and seizing the opportunities of the moment. Our approach to sustainable design takes advantage of what is readily available and explores what is practically achievable.

The Social Factor

Toilets, especially in rural communities, stand for more than just a bodily routine. It is also an opportunity for socialization. Our toilet provides space for men and women to socialize while waiting, promoting better hygienic habits built around social closeness.

Healthy Living

Provision for selling health care products like contraceptives, sanitary napkins & other low cost hygiene product through shops & vending machines; along with disposing machine for used ones.

MAINTENANCE MODEL

Almost all of today’s public toilets share one common problem, and it’s not hard to guess what it is. Maintenance of public toilets is one of the reasons that drives people away from them. Intentions, no matter how good they are, need practical considerations. Our toilet does just that. It requires the least maintenance by incorporating maintenance as a core, fundamental feature of the design and not just a side-step. Water tank filling, monitoring the health of the treatment system, cleaning activities and maintenance of mechanical and electrical fttiings are all a part of our maintenance model.

ECO SERIES

Eco-Mitra

An exemplary solution to the most scathing problem of sanitation in India. It is specifically designed for Indian conditions and public and equipped with all the latest technologies.

Eco-Junior 'B'

This unit specially designed for school, incorporates combination of urinals and WC for boys. Its design tries to imbibe the peaceful nature of schools.

Eco-Junior 'G'

In the same series, this is designed keeping in mind the special privacy needs of girls. The common space is thoughtfully placed with proper dayight and ventilation.

Eco-Lat

The space efficient design makes every small inch of a space count. It is ideal for places plagued by overcrowding and inadequate provision of sanitation services.
